About Me

Hello, my name is Rui Margarido, and I am a Computer Engineering student with extensive experience in Mechanical Engineering. Throughout my academic and professional journey, I have cultivated a deep interest in technology. As a mechanical engineer, I had the opportunity to work with advanced technologies, such as lasers for tube and sheet cutting, as well as in the industrialization of complex products and in production lines with a high level of automation. I am convinced that the combination of my skills in Mechanical Engineering with the knowledge I am acquiring in Computer Engineering uniquely positions me to tackle the challenges of an increasingly interconnected and digitalized job market. With this diverse background, I am prepared to contribute significantly to projects that require an integrated approach, bringing together the best of both engineering disciplines.

Study of the algorithms of the Euler method and the 4th order Runge-Kutta method

The objective of this project is to develop a Java application that models the epidemic consumption of drugs using a system of first-order ordinary differential equations (ODEs). The SIR model (Susceptible, Infected, Recovered) was used to represent the dynamics of drug consumption within a population. Numerical methods, specifically the Euler and 4th order Runge-Kutta methods, were employed to solve the ODEs. Finally, a comparison of the results obtained from both methods was conducted.
